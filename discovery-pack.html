Day-in-the-Life — 12 Cross-Cutting Themes
Source: 60 recordings · 17h 24m · Commercial, Finance, Marketing, Personal Injury, Scheduling & Ops
T1 — Email & phone are the integration layer
Outlook/Gmail used as source of truth for case context, handoffs, client comms, scheduling requests, invoices, and follow-up across all functional areas.
T2 — Swivel-chair verification dominates knowledge work
Every topic shows users toggling between 2-6 systems to validate the same case, party, invoice, rate, calendar, or document data.
T3 — Batch work performed one row at a time
Scheduling confirmations, judge availability entries, eBilling sends, portal statement pushes, conflict-check searches — all through single-item interactions.
T4 — Diaries, inboxes & spreadsheets substitute for task management
Commercial uses diaries as primary task queue. Finance prints deleted-invoice pages for follow-up. Teams use inbox folders or spreadsheets to track unresolved work.
T5 — Business rules live in people's heads
CA simultaneous invoice send, $0/negative deletion, express-rate-discount admin-fee logic, special PI rates, full-day fee handling, PI roster caps, bifurcated neutral rates.
T6 — Search/list views hide decision-critical fields
Marketing can't see rates, city, case type, deactivation, or roster eligibility. Scheduling can't see day-of-week or date restrictions in grid views.
T7 — Data-model mismatches leak into daily work
Pro se parties need duplicate firm/client records. Multi-state neutrals need duplicate profiles. Bifurcated rates live in notes. Prepaid ledgers need fee overrides.
T8 — Status transitions are manual compensating controls
Users manually flip invoice, DRA, party, signed, waiting, confirmed, paid, and scanning statuses — then create diaries to verify someone else completed the next step.
T9 — Intake completeness drives downstream rework
Missing respondent details, adjusters, claim numbers, office locations, wrong case types, duplicate submissions all create phone calls, eCourts lookups, rejections, and delayed invoices.
T10 — Performance & hard limits amplify manual toil
eBilling's 20-invoice cap, Scheduling's 3-hold limit, one-date availability entry, and slow 500+ case report exports increase the cost of already-repetitive workflows.
T11 — Document generation & storage are fragmented
Commercial finds templates from prior cases and multiple drives. PI forwards agreements for scanning. Finance combines billing PDFs. Scheduling relies on generated confirmations.
T12 — External authoritative systems manually consulted
Marketing checks bar websites. PI checks NY State eCourts. Finance prepares Acumatica exports. Scheduling uses Outlook/Google/myADR calendars. All manual lookup, copy, and re-entry.

⚠️ Where work escapes the system (Cross-Cutting Theme T1, T2)
Phase 1: Intake via Outlook/Gmail — case numbers copied manually into CMS. Commercial: no portal at all.
Phase 2: DRA correspondence generated in Word outside system. Signed agreements forwarded for manual scanning.
Phase 3: Neutral rosters built in Word. Conflict checks across 4 separate system searches. Bios hunted from OneDrive.
Phase 4: Confirmations sent via Outlook 1-at-a-time. Calendar holds duplicated across Outlook + Google + NAM + myADR.
Phase 5: Decision documents emailed externally (security risk flagged by Mike in session).
Phase 6: Invoice review in Excel. Reconciliation across 3+ reports. Cash app manual. Collections 100% manual.
Phase 7: Client status reports built in Excel. No self-service — IT runs queries for most meaningful reports.
